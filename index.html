History of Previous Events

1896: First Modern Olympics

The first modern Olympic Games were held in Athens, Greece, with 14 nations participating. This was the revival of the ancient Greek Olympic Games after more than 1,500 years.

1936: Berlin Olympics

The Berlin Games saw Jesse Owens, an African American athlete, winning 4 gold medals, challenging Nazi ideology of racial superiority.The Nazi regime used the Olympics to showcase their regime to the world.

1968: Mexico City

During the medal ceremony, Tommie Smith and John Carlos gave the Black Power salute, becoming an iconic moment of protest against racial inequality,That event was the first to use a synthetic track for training and events.

1980: Moscow Olympics Boycott

The United States led a boycott of the Moscow Olympics in protest of the Soviet Union's invasion of Afghanistan, with many countries refusing to participate.

1996: Centennial Olympic Games

The Atlanta Games marked the 100th anniversary of the modern Olympics, highlighted by Muhammad Ali lighting the Olympic torch.

2008: Beijing Olympics

Beijing hosted one of the most spectacular Olympics ever, with China leading the medal count and the opening ceremony becoming a defining moment in Olympic history.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Olympics is a global event featuring summer and winter sports, where thousands of athletes participate in a variety of competitions.

The Olympic Games are held every four years, alternating between Summer and Winter Games every two years.

Athletes from around the world can participate in the Olympics, provided they meet the qualifications for their respective sports.

The Olympic motto is "Citius, Altius, Fortius," which means "Faster, Higher, Stronger."

Host cities are selected by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) through a rigorous bidding process.
